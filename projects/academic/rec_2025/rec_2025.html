February 2025 - May 2025

SolidWorks Manufacturing Prototyping Fabrication ASTM Safety Standards Technical Writing

Project Overview

The Ride Engineering Competition (REC) offers an opportunity for students to demonstrate real-world engineering practices within the ride engineering and design industry. The project lasted six months, which incorporated planning, design, and manufacturing a small attraction based on candy Starbursts as “riders.” The competition team was composed of members from the University of Colorado Theme Park Engineering and Design Club. The team was divided into three sub-teams: mechanical, electrical, and theming.

The team decided on designing a double pendulum ride that had two vehicles that swing opposite directions of each other. This competition is hosted by The Ohio State University in Columbus, Ohio. This put extra considerations on the design as it needed to be disassembled and packed into carry-on and checked luggage for travel.

My Contributions

  • Led development of the pendulum arms, including concept refinement and design ownership.
    • Researched materials and performed strength analysis to guide selection of 1" PVC arms.
  • Supported manufacturing and system integration during the final build phase.
    • Volunteered 60 hours during the week of manufacturing, leading up to the competition.
    • Aided with assembling the full frame and pendulum mechanism, then assisted the electrical team with final integration.
  • Supported on-site competition operations in Ohio.
    • Assembled the ride from 14 modular pieces into a fully functional system under time constraints.
    • Performed maintenance and mechanical repairs during the live six-hour competition window.

Results

  • 60 hours contributed during final manufacturing week
  • 14-piece modular design assembled on-site
  • 30-minute setup window achieved for competition preparation
  • 3.5 hours of runtime during a 6-hour competition period
