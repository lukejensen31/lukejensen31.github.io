Design for Manufacturability & Assembly Course Project

August 2024 - December 2024

SolidWorks Technical Writing Team Collaboration DFMA

Project Overview

This course provided an in-depth introduction to Design for Manufacturability and Assembly (DFMA) principles used to minimize product cost through informed design and process decisions. Two hald-semester projects were completed:

  • Reverse Engineering Project: Analysis of an existing product to understand design intent and manufacturing decisions.
  • Novel Design Project: Development and analysis of a novel design and then redesigning it using DFMA principles to improve manufacturability and assembly efficiency.

Both projects emphasized evaluating design choices through material selection, manufacturing processes, assembly efficiency, and cost analysis.

My Contributions

  • Collaborated with a team of four engineers across both projects.
    • Reverse engineering analysis on an Xbox One controller.
    • Novel design analysis for a new style countertop grain dispenser.
  • Analysis for both projects included:
    • Patent search on both designs.
    • Component Analysis.
    • Material analysis using Ashby charts.
    • Manufacturing process analysis.
    • Assembly process analysis.
    • Economic analysis.
  • Utilized SolidWorks throughout the semester.
    • Modeled real-world assemblies using physical measurements.
    • Designed novel components that interfaced with teammates-designed parts.
    • Created detailed technical drawings for manufacturing and assembly.

Results

  • Delivered complete engineering reports and presentations for both projects.
  • Demonstrated measurable DFMA improvements through reduced part cound, simplified assmbly steps, and improved manufacturability.
  • Communicated technical findings and design tradeoffs to the professor and classmates.
  • Gained hands-on experience applying DFMA tools to both existing and novel product designs.
