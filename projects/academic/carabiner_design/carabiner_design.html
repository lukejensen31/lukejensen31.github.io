Advanced Product Design Course Project - Carabiner Design

August 2024 - December 2024

SolidWorks Technical Writing Team Collaboration Website Design Davinci Resolve Video Editing Human Centered Design

Project Overview

Throughout the Advanced Product Design course, the full design process was explored from early concept development through user testing and final prototyping. A strong emphasis was placed on human-centered design and the importance of “designing the right thing,” rather than immediately converging on a solution.

The project began with a broad identification of a need area, after which teams of four engineers selected a specific focus and completed two full iterative cycles of the design process. Continuous user feedback, interviews, and testing played a central role in shaping design decisions throughout the semester.

My Contributions

  • Collaborated with a team of four engineers to design a product within the climbing equipment domain.
  • Contributed to concept ideation through multiple structured brainstorming and refinement sessions.
  • Organized and conducted interviews with sixteen climbers across a range of experience levels.
  • Developed a project website to document and communicate the progression of the design process.
  • Built content creation and video editing skills using DaVinci Resolve to produce project media, including:
    • User interview summary videos explaining design decisions.
    • Prototype usage and testing videos highlighting successes and failures.
    • An advertisement-style video showcasing the final prototype concept.
  • Prototyped and tested multiple design iterations to verify that key performance indicators were met.

Results

  • Completed two full cycles of the human-centered design process informed by direct user feedback.
  • Validated design decisions through interviews, usability testing, and prototype evaluation.
  • Produced a final prototype supported by documented user insights and performance metrics.
  • Strengthened skills in user research, communication, and iterative design alongside technical prototyping.
