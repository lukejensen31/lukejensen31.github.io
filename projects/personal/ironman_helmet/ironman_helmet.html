Ironman Helmet Build - Mechatronics Course Project

August 2025 - December 2025

3D Printing 3D Scanning Prototyping Fabrication Integration Finishing & Painting Arduino

Project Overview

This graduate mechatronics project focused on the integration of mechanical, electronic, and software systems into a single wearable device. A 3D printed Iron Man helmet was selected as the platform to implement a servo-driven faceplate inspired by the motion seen in the films.

The helmet was fabricated using FDM 3D printing and incorporated a custom linkage mechanism, micro servos, an Arduino Nano microcontroller, and LED illumination. The project emphasized iterative design, benchtop testing, and system-level integration while working within tight spatial constraints.

My Contributions

  • Fabricated the helmet using FDM 3D printing and performed test fitting to evaluate wearability, clearances, and mechanism integration.
  • Used Blender to digitally model, simulate, and refine linkage geometry and pivot points to resolve mechanical binding and interference.
  • Integrated dual MG90S micro servos, an Arduino Nano, and button-based input to control faceplate motion.
  • Developed embedded control logic using the VarSpeedServo and OneButton libraries to enable smooth motion and multi-function single-button control.
  • Designed and implemented LED eye illumination with movie-accurate flicker effects synchronized to faceplate motion.
  • Performed benchtop electronics testing followed by full system integration within the helmet.
  • Completed post-processing, surface finishing, painting, and final assembly to produce a wearable and visually accurate final helmet.

Results

  • Successfully demonstrated a functional servo-driven faceplate with smooth, repeatable motion.
  • Implemented reliable single-button control for opening and closing the faceplate.
  • Integrated LED eye illumination that automatically responds to helmet state.
  • Delivered a fully wearable, visually finished prototype combining mechanical, electronic, and software systems.
  • Identified opportunities for future improvements, including optimized linkage geometries, alternative actuation methods, and higher-performance materials.
