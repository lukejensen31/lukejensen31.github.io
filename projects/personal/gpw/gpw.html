1943 GPW WWII Jeep Restoration

May 2020 - May 2024

Vehicle Restoration Drivetrain Rebuild Welding Fabrication Automotive Bodywork HVLP Painting

Project Overview

During World War II, Willys-Overland Motors was awarded a contract with the U.S. Army to produce a light utility vehicle to mobilize military forces. As demand rapidly increased, Ford Motor Company was subcontracted to assist with production. By the end of the war, Willys and Ford produced approximately 363,000 and 280,000 Jeeps respectively.

In 1970, my father purchased a Ford GPW at the age of fourteen as his first vehicle. Over decades of use, including off-road driving and a rollover, the vehicle accumulated significant wear and tear. Eventually, the GPW was parked for 45 years when replacement parts were unavailable.

In May of 2020, my father and I began a full restoration of the vehicle. The project spanned four years and concluded with a fully functional, road ready 1943 Ford GPW.

My Contributions

  • Disassembled the GPW to the bare frame to enable structural repairs and complete system rebuilds, including:
    • Frame repair and correction of prior damage over a summer break.
    • Full drivetrain rebuild and reinstallation onto the restored frame.
    • Design and fabrication of a custom temporary seating mechanism to enable test driving prior to body installation.
  • Fabricated and welded new steel panels to repair rusted body sections, including:
    • Complete rear floor and tailgate replacement.
    • Use of spot welding techniques to integrate new panels while preserving surrounding original bodywork
  • Performed automotive bodywork to prepare components for final finish:
    • Corrected surface imperfections using automotive body fillers.
    • Prepared and painted all components using HVLP spray guns.
    • Applied automotive-grade paint systems for durability and finish.
  • Reassembled the GPW to a historically accurate configuration while ensuring full mechanical functionality.

Results

  • Successfully completed a full ground-up restoration of a 1943 Ford GPW Jeep.
  • Returned the vehicle to a drivable, reliable, and historically accurate condition.
  • Developed hands-on skills in mechanical systems, welding, fabrication, automotive bodywork, and painting.
  • Completed a multi-year restoration project.
  • Received first and second place awards at multiple car shows.
