Academic Projects

Projects completed as part of coursework and club activities.

Ride Engineering Competition - 2025
Contributed to the design and manufacturing of a double-pendulum flat ride for the 2025 Ride Engineering Competition (REC). The design followed ASTM safety standards and focused on balancing structural performance, manufacturability, and operational reliability for a live competition environment.
Design for Manufacturing and Assembly (DFMA)
Design for Manufacturability and Assembly (DFMA) course project focused on reverse engineering a game controller and redesigning a grain dispenser to improve its manufacturability and assembly efficiency using DFMA principles.
Advanced Product Design Course Project - Carabiner Design
A semester-long product design project focused on human-centered design, emphasizing iterative prototyping, user interviews, and testing to ensure the final solution addressed real user needs within the climbing equipment space.
Mechatronics Course Project - Mario Kart
A semester-long project within a team of seven to design and build an autonomous driving vehicle capable of racing and ideally picking up dice, inspired by the Mario Kart video game.
